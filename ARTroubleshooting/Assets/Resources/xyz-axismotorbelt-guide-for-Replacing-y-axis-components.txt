# Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly Replacement Guide

This guide provides detailed instructions for replacing the Y-axis component on the Bambu Lab A1 printer.

## Applicable Model

- **Bambu Lab A1**

## When to Use This Guide

- **Abnormal noise and sluggishness on the Y-axis**
- **Recommended by Bambu Lab customer support**

## Tools and Materials Needed

- **New Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly**
- **H2.0 hex wrench**
- **Estimated Time: 25 minutes**

## List of Screws

- **Cable bracket BT3-27**
- **Bottom cover screws BT3-8 (16 PCS)**
- **Ground cable M3-6 (2 PCS)**
- **Heatbed M3-6 (2 PCS)**
- **Y-axis middle screws M3-23 (8 PCS)**
- **Y-axis both ends screws BT3-23 (4 PCS)**

## Safety Warning

**It's crucial to power off the printer** before conducting any maintenance work, including work on the printer's electronics and tool head wires. Performing tasks with the printer on can result in a short circuit, leading to electronic damage and safety hazards. **Turn off the printer and disconnect it from the power source** before conducting any maintenance. For any concerns or questions about following this guide, **open a new ticket in our Support Page**.

# Remove the Old Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly

### Step 1: Prepare the Printer
#### Step 1: Place the Printer on Its Front
1. Flip the screen to the side.
2. Gently place the printer on its front, using a box of filament to keep the top side raised to avoid pressure on the extruder and screen.

### Step 2: Disconnect the Cable and Remove the Cable Bracket
#### Step 1: Loosen and Remove Cables
1. Loosen the yellow-marked screws (do not completely remove).
2. Disconnect the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd cables (camera, Z-axis motor, X-axis motor).
3. Push up the cable bracket to disconnect the USB-C cable.
   
   **Note:** If the cable bracket is difficult to remove, completely remove the yellow-marked screws (BT3-27) and try again.

### Step 3: Remove the Printer's Bottom Cover
#### Step 1: Remove Screws and Cover
1. Remove the 16 screws (BT3-8) at the bottom.
2. Lift off the printer bottom cover.

### Step 4: Remove the Bottom Screws and the Y Motor Cable
#### Step 1: Remove Ground and Heat Sink Screws
1. Remove the two **fixing screws** on the ground cable (M3-6).
2. Remove the **fixing screws for the heat sink** and disconnect the Y motor cable.
3. Remove the cables from the routing trough.

### Step 5: Remove the Heatbed and the Y-axis Cover Plate
#### Step 1: Prepare for Removal
1. Place the printer on a flat surface and raise the X-axis to the top.
2. Move the heatbed to the bottom and remove the Y-axis cover.

   **Note:** Use the H2.0 hex wrench to remove the 4 screws fixing the heatbed (M3-6) before lifting it off.

### Step 6: Remove the Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly
#### Step 1: Remove Screws and Assembly
1. Move the Y-axis slide block to the front and remove the 8 red-marked screws (M3-23) and 2 green-marked screws (BT3-23).
2. Move the pulley to the tail end and remove the remaining 2 green-marked screws (BT3-23).
3. Remove the fixing screws of the tail motor.
   
   **Note:** If the assembly is glued, clean off the remaining glue before removal.

# Install the New Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly

### Step 1: Install the New Y-axis Linear Guide Assembly
#### Step 1: Position and Secure the Assembly
1. Pass the Y motor cable through from the bottom.
2. Insert a section of the tensioner into the base and install the Y motor end.
3. Lock the black screw at the Y motor position to confirm correct placement.
4. Install four green-marked screws at both ends and eight red-marked screws at the middle position (M3-23 and BT3-23).

### Step 2: Connect the Y Motor Cable and the Bottom Screws
#### Step 1: Route and Connect Cable
1. Pass the Y motor cable through the bottom wiring trough.
2. Install two ground cable fixing screws (M3-6).
3. Connect the Y motor connector and tighten the fixing screws of the heat sink.

### Step 3: Install the Printer Bottom Cover
#### Step 1: Secure the Bottom Cover
1. Ensure the position of the cable bracket for the motor cable is correct.
2. Insert the cable connector and install the bottom cover.
3. Lock in the 16 screws (BT3-8), being careful not to over-tighten as they are self-tapping screws.

### Step 4: Install the Wire Bracket and Connect the Wires
#### Step 1: Align and Secure the Bracket
1. Carefully align the wire bracket and push it downwards, ensuring the USB cable is fully pushed against the printer frame.
2. Tighten the screw (BT3-27) to lock it in place.
3. Re-connect the Camera, Motor X, and Motor Z cables.

### Step 5: Install the Heatbed Component and the Y-axis Cover Plate
#### Step 1: Align and Secure the Heatbed
1. Align the heatbed metallic frame with the two metallic stands on the Y-axis.
2. Install the four screws (M3-6) on the heatbed.
3. Move the heatbed forward, pass the Y-axis cover from beneath through, and push it forward to snap it in place.

# Printer Calibration

**Calibrate the device** before starting actual printing to ensure the Y-axis component replacement does not affect the normal operation of the printer.
