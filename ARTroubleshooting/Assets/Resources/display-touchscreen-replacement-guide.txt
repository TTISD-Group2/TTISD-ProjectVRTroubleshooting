# Step-by-Step Guide to Replace the Screen on the A1

### When to Use This Guide
This guide should be used if the screen of the A1 is faulty and needs replacement. Common issues include:
- Screen not detecting interaction
- Screen not turning on
- Recommended by Bambu Lab Customer Support

### Tools and Materials Needed
- **Bambu Lab Screen for A1**
- **H2.0 Allen Key**
- **H1.5 Allen Key**
- **Flat End Screwdriver or Prying Tool**
- **Sharp Pliers**
- **Estimated Time: 20-30 minutes**

### Safety Warning
**IMPORTANT:** Always power off the printer and disconnect it from the power source before starting maintenance work to prevent short circuits and other electronic damage.

For any concerns or questions, please [open a new ticket on our Support Page](#).

### Steps to Remove the Old Screen

#### Step 1: Place the Printer on Its Front
1. Flip the printer on its side.
2. Gently place the printer on its front using a box of filament to support the top side and avoid pressure on the extruder and screen.

#### Step 2: Disconnect the Wires and Remove the Wire Bracket
1. Disconnect the cables of the camera, X-axis motor, and Z-axis motor.
2. Loosen **1 screw**.
3. Push the cable bracket upwards, disconnect the USB data cable, and remove the bracket.

#### Step 3: Remove the Printer's Bottom Cover
1. Remove **10 screws** (marked in red) to remove the bottom cover.

#### Step 4: Disconnect the Screen Ribbon Cable
1. Carefully raise the black plastic clip on the mainboard to release the ribbon cable.
2. Remove the ribbon cable from the board.

#### Step 5: Remove the Screen Clip
1. Remove **2 screws** holding the screen metallic clip.
2. Use sharp pliers to pull the metallic clip towards you with slight resistance.

#### Step 6: Remove the Screen from the Printer
1. With the clip removed, carefully remove the screen. Note the alignment of the plastic tabs in its holder for correct removal.

===PAGE_BREAK===

### Install the New Screen

#### Step 1: Attach the Screen to the Printer
1. Slide the screen halfway through the holder, ensuring the plastic tabs are aligned as shown in the guide.

#### Step 2: Install the Metallic Bracket
1. Align the metallic bracket as depicted.
2. Install **2 screws** to hold the bracket in place.
3. Use a flat end screwdriver to help slide the screen into the bracket until it clicks into place.

#### Step 3: Attach the Screen Ribbon Cable
1. Gently insert the ribbon cable into the motherboard connector.
2. Lock the black plastic clip to secure the cable, ensuring the white line on the ribbon cable is aligned.

#### Step 4: Install the Printer's Bottom Cover
1. Manage the motor wire on the top left side as indicated.
2. Clip the cover in place and install **10 screws**, making sure not to over-tighten.

#### Step 5: Install the Wire Bracket and Connect the Wires
1. Align and push the wire bracket downwards, ensuring the USB cable is fully against the frame.
2. Tighten the screw to lock the bracket in place.
3. Reconnect the camera, X-axis motor, and Z-axis motor cables.

### Verify the Functionality
Turn on the printer and interact with the screen to ensure proper functionality.

### Calibration Step After the Operation
No calibration step is required after this operation.

### Potential Problems and Solutions
- **Camera Not Working:** Check the camera wire installation as described in Step 5.
- **X-axis / Z-axis Not Working:** Ensure the X and Z motor wires are correctly installed as in Step 5.
- **Screen Displays an Error Message:** Check the USB cable connection as in Step 5.
- **Screen Not Turning On/Touch Not Detected:** Confirm the ribbon cable installation as in Step 3.
