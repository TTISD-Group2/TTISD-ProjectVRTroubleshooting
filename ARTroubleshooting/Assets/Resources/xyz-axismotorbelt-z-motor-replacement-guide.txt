# Step-by-Step Guide to Remove and Install the Z-axis Motor for Bambu Lab A1 Printer

### Introduction

This guide provides detailed steps to remove and replace the Z-axis motor in the Bambu Lab A1 printer. Follow each step carefully to ensure a safe and successful maintenance procedure.

### Safety Warning

**Important!** Always **power off** the printer and **disconnect it from the power source** before conducting any maintenance. This prevents electrical shorts, damage to the electronics, and ensures your safety.

### Required Tools and Materials

- **Z-axis motor for Bambu Lab A1** (Contact Customer Support for purchase)
- **H2.0 hex wrench**
- **Estimated time**: 20 minutes

===PAGE_BREAK===

### Remove Z-axis Motor

#### Remove the Y-axis Top Cover
1. Stand the printer upright and raise the X-axis to the top position.
2. Push the heatbed forward and remove the Y-axis Top Cover.

#### Loosen the Z-axis Belt Tension Screws
1. Use the **H2.0 hex wrench** to loosen the Z-belt tension screws by one turn. **Do not remove completely.**
   - The tension screws are located at the base against the right column, visible when the heatbed is moved forward.

#### Disconnect the Cables
1. Lay the printer on its left side to expose the cable box on the bottom.
2. Disconnect the camera cable, X motor cable, and Z-motor cable in sequence.
3. Loosen a silver screw, slide the cable box to disconnect the USB cable, then open the Z-motor cable cover and remove the Z-motor cable.

#### Remove the Bottom Base Assembly
1. Move the heatbed forward and remove **10 screws** using the **H2.0 hex wrench**.
2. Push the heatbed backward to the bottom and remove **2 screws** with the **H2.0 hex wrench**.
3. Lift up one side of the base and remove the entire base.

#### Remove the Metal Base Plate
1. Unscrew **8 long screws** that lock the Z-axis column using the **H2.0 hex wrench**.
2. Remove **4 short screws** at the middle part of the metal base plate.

#### Remove the Z-Motor
1. Remove the tapes.
2. Unscrew **4 screws** with the **H2.0 hex key**, then remove the Z-Motor, Z Belt, and driving wheel.
   - It is recommended to attach a corner of the tape to the table for reuse during reinstallation.

===PAGE_BREAK===

### Install the Z-Motor

#### Raise the X-axis
1. Raise the X-axis to the top position, ensuring both left and right sides are pushed to the top to avoid skewing.

#### Install the Z-Motor
1. Install the driving wheel onto the new Z motor.
2. Arrange the Z Belt according to the motion path, pass the Z-motor through the Z Belt, and install it in place, locking in the **4 screws**.
3. Arrange the motor cable and secure it with tapes.

#### Install the Metal Base Plate
1. Install the metal base plate, ensuring the correct installation direction according to the screw positions.
2. Lock **4 short screws** marked orange in the middle part of the base plate, paying attention to their correct positioning.

#### Install the Bottom Base Assembly
1. Keep the Printer Frame upright and carefully install the bottom base assembly onto it, avoiding the Z-motor cable.
2. Move the heatbed forward to its furthest position and securely fasten **10 screws**.
3. Then, move the heatbed backward to its furthest position and tighten **2 screws**.

#### Connect the Cables
1. Place the printer in a backward position and connect the Z-motor cable to the cable management box.
2. Install the cable management box onto the bottom base, slide it along the slot direction, connect the USB cable, and tighten one silver screw.
3. Follow the text prompts and color labels to connect the X motor cable, Z motor cable, and camera cable. Organize the cables properly to ensure they are not being pressed or tangled.

#### Install Y-axis Top Cover
1. Place the printer upright and move the heatbed forward to its furthest position.
2. Take the Y-axis Top Cover and pass it over through the bottom of the heatbed. Push the cover forward until it reaches the end, ensuring that the cover aligns with the clips.
3. Install the cover in place by securing it onto the clips.
   - **Note**: If the heatbed is not moved all the way to the end, the Y-axis Top Cover may get stuck. Manually lift the Y-axis cover slightly to assist in its installation.

#### Tension the Z-belt
1. Turn on the printer and perform a homing process via the control screen.
2. After homing completes, press the up and down buttons on the right only once respectively, to move the X-axis up and down once.
   - **Ensure the nozzle does not touch the heatbed before moving the X-axis down.**
3. Use the **H2.0 hex wrench** to tighten the 2 tension screws.

#### Calibration Step
1. In the Calibration screen, select only "Vibration Compensation," and then tap Start.
   - The calibration will confirm the printer is running as expected.
